Exposure:
Blood lead levels measured during pregnancy and early childhood.

Health outcome:
Neurodevelopmental outcomes, including cognitive and behavioral scores.

Key findings:
Higher prenatal and early-life lead exposure was associated with lower cognitive test scores in children.
Associations were more pronounced among children from socioeconomically disadvantaged households.

Conclusion:
Early-life lead exposure may adversely affect neurodevelopment.
